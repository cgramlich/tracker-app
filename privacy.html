Priority Captain

Privacy Policy

Effective July 22, 2026

Priority Captain is a personal planning app: you capture tasks, projects, goals, and ideas, organize them by the areas of your life, and optionally see your real calendar alongside them. It is owned and operated by MilSpo Life LLC, a Texas limited liability company based in Dallas, Texas (“we,” “us”). This policy explains what information the app handles, why, and the choices you have. The short version: your data is yours. We use it to run the app for you. We do not sell it, and we do not show ads.

Information we collect

  • Account information. Your email address and a password. Passwords are stored only as secure cryptographic hashes by our authentication provider. We never see or store your plain password.
  • Your content. The tasks, projects, goals, ideas, notes, and settings you add, including the categories, locations, Spaces, and templates you set up. This content is private to your account.
  • Calendar connections. If you connect a calendar, the private feed address you provide (an .ics link) is stored on our server so we can fetch your events for you. This address is treated as a secret: it is never sent back to your browser and never shown to you again after you add it.
  • AI usage records. When you use an AI feature we record that a call happened, its size (token counts), and its estimated cost. We use this to enforce fair usage limits and protect the app from abuse.
  • Technical logs. Standard server logs, such as request timestamps and status codes, used to keep the app healthy and debug problems.

We do not collect advertising identifiers, we do not use analytics trackers, and we do not buy data about you from anyone.

How we use your information

  • To provide the app: storing your data, syncing it across your devices, and showing it back to you.
  • To fetch and display your connected calendars, when you add them.
  • To power the AI features you choose to use (described below).
  • To enforce usage limits, prevent abuse, and keep costs sustainable.
  • To respond when you contact us for support.

Your calendars

Connecting a calendar is optional. When you add one, you give us its private .ics feed address, and our server fetches your events from it so the app can show your schedule next to your tasks. Two things are true by design:

  • The view is read-only. We never create, change, or delete anything on your calendar.
  • The feed address stays on our server. It is not stored in the app on your device, not included in the data that syncs to your browser, and not written to logs in a readable form. If you remove the calendar, we stop fetching it.

Fetched events are held briefly in a short-lived cache so the view loads quickly, and are refreshed from your calendar provider.

AI features

Some features send data to Anthropic (our AI provider) for processing, and only when you use them: organizing a brain-dump sends the text you entered; scanning a photo of a list sends that image; the calendar assistant sends your question and the relevant event details; and the daily brief sends a summary of your tasks and today’s schedule. The results come back to your app. Photos you scan are used to read the text on them and are not stored. Under Anthropic’s API terms, this data is not used to train their models by default.

Service providers

We use a small set of providers to run Priority Captain. Each receives only what it needs to do its job:

  • Supabase – database and authentication (where your account and your data live).
  • Railway – hosts our backend server.
  • Anthropic – AI processing for the features above.
  • GitHub Pages – serves the app itself.
  • Your calendar providers – when you connect a calendar (for example Google Calendar or Outlook), our server fetches your events from the address you gave us.

We do not use payment processors, advertising networks, or location or search providers. Password reset emails are sent through our authentication provider to the address on your account.

Sharing

Priority Captain has no public sharing or publishing feature. Your content is visible only to you, through your own signed-in account. Other people using the app cannot see it. If you want your own copy of your data, you can export it from the app at any time.

Data storage and security

Your data is stored in our hosted database, isolated per account. Connections are encrypted in transit (HTTPS), access to your data requires your authenticated session, and provider credentials are kept on our servers, never in the app on your device. The app keeps a copy of your session and a local cache on your device so it loads fast and works offline; signing out clears them.

Retention and deletion

We keep your content for as long as your account is active so it is there when you come back. To delete your account and its data, email us at support@menucaptain.com and we will remove it within 30 days. (In-app account deletion is coming; until then, email works.)

Children

Priority Captain is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

AI accuracy

The AI features produce drafts you review before anything is saved, and answers based on your own data. They can be wrong or incomplete. Check anything that matters before you rely on it.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we will update this page and its effective date. Continued use of the app after a change means you accept the updated policy.
